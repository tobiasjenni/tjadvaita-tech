Background

Medicine, land,
and unconditional love.

Tobias was born in Switzerland in the early nineties, and medicine was his first language. He trained as a nurse and spent years working across Swiss hospitals — emergency wards, intensive care, the kind of clinical environments where you learn quickly what the body can withstand and what it cannot. He was good at it. But the longer he worked inside the Western medical system, the harder it became to ignore what he was seeing: a machine built not around health, but around profit. Patients managed, not healed. Symptoms treated, causes ignored.

That recognition became a turning point. He left the clinical world not out of disillusionment, but out of conviction. If medicine was going to mean anything, it had to go deeper.

His first step into the holistic space came through medical cannabis. He co-founded one of Switzerland's first medical cannabis associations and later a production company — working directly with patients suffering from autoimmune diseases, cancer, chronic conditions that conventional medicine had run out of answers for. The results were undeniable. This wasn't fringe work. It was medicine doing what medicine is supposed to do.

From there, Tobias moved deeper into longevity, ancestral health, plant medicine ceremony and the energetic dimensions of the body that clinical training never touched. He has spent over a decade in these fields — not as a tourist, but as a builder. Today he is developing a health and movement centre on Koh Phangan called 258, a space dedicated to body, movement and spirit, with a music studio and creative hub as part of its vision. He is also building Magic Land — a co-village and retreat sanctuary on the same island — alongside Julia and a small core team.

In parallel, for the past five years he has operated his own Fintech platform, giving individuals and businesses worldwide access to banking, crypto and payment infrastructure across 115+ countries. The same systems thinking that runs through his medical and land work runs through this: build structures that hold, so people inside them can be free.

"Love, in its most unconditional form, is the actual substance of everything I do."

In Human Design, Tobias is a Generator — a being of pure life force, here not to chase but to respond. When something genuinely lights him up, the energy is unmistakable and self-sustaining. His Incarnation Cross is the Vessel of Love — self-love, love of humanity, love of the body, universal love. This is not a romantic designation. It means that love is the actual engine of everything he builds: the health work, the land, the ceremonies, the Fintech. A refusal to let people go without what they need.

He carries the name Jeronimo — after the Apache warrior who never surrendered. It suits him. He lives between Koh Phangan and Nepal, and builds as if both matter equally.
